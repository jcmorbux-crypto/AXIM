A

Getting Started

AXIM watches Telegram channels for trading signals, sizes each trade using rules you control, and places trades on Pocket Option automatically. Nothing trades until you tell it to.

First launch

  1. Create the Owner account - the first account on a fresh install becomes the Owner automatically, with full access.
  2. Follow the Setup Wizard (linked from Settings > General any time you want to re-run it). It walks you through everything below in order.
  3. Connect Telegram - links AXIM to the Telegram account that will receive signals.
  4. Connect Pocket Option - starts AXIM's background service, which opens a browser session to Pocket Option.
  5. Create a Fund - a Fund is a bankroll (see Section C). Everything you do lives inside one.
  6. Create or choose a money management profile - how much AXIM risks per trade (see Section F).
  7. Select Signal Sources - which Telegram channels AXIM should actually act on.
  8. Start a Demo session and watch a real trade go through the whole pipeline, risk-free.
B

Mission Control

Your home screen. One status line tells you if AXIM is healthy; everything else follows from there.

What each card means

  • Status line (top) - "Running normally" means AXIM is connected to both Telegram and Pocket Option and watching your selected sources. "Reconnecting" usually clears itself within a minute. "Not running" means the background service is stopped.
  • Fund selector (top right) - switch between "All Funds (combined)" and any single Fund to see that portfolio's own numbers instead of the global total.
  • Today's Performance - the day's realized profit/loss, win rate, and trade count (or a Fund's lifetime numbers, if one is selected).
  • Risk - a one-line read of whether you're within your limits, with the actual numbers available on click.
  • Recent Activity - your latest signals and their outcomes.
  • System diagnostics (bottom, collapsed) - technical detail (process status, connection health) for troubleshooting. You don't need to look at this in normal use.

Starting, pausing, and stopping

Start Session takes you to Trading Sessions to configure and launch a session. Pause keeps AXIM connected but stops it acting on new signals - use this if you want to step away briefly without fully stopping.

What Emergency Stop does

Emergency Stop immediately halts every signal from being requested or executed, across your whole account, and ends the active session. It is deliberately available to every logged-in user, not just Owners/Admins - if something looks wrong, anyone should be able to hit the brakes. Clear it from the same button once you're ready to resume.

C

Multi-Fund Manager

A Fund is a separate bankroll with its own balance, its own Pocket Option account, sources, strategy, and history - AXIM doesn't assume you only ever run one pool of money on one account. Use separate Funds to keep a Demo bankroll apart from a Live one, run two different Pocket Option accounts side by side, or keep an aggressive strategy's results apart from a conservative one.

Trading Balance vs. Protected Balance

Trading Balance is the money actually available for AXIM to size new trades against. Protected Balance (shown in gold) is money the Profit Vault has skimmed off and set aside - it no longer affects position sizing, on purpose, so a good run doesn't put your whole balance back at risk. Total Account Value is simply the two added together - vaulting moves money into a safer bucket, it never destroys it.

Creating a Fund

Go to Multi-Fund Manager > New Fund, give it a name and a starting balance. From the Fund's detail view you can then attach a Pocket Option account (see "Connecting a broker account" below), assign Signal Sources (which Telegram channels feed it), and set a default money management profile (used automatically when you start a session under this Fund, unless you override it).

Connecting a broker account

Each Fund trades through one Pocket Option account, attached from the Fund's detail view: pick an account from the Broker Account dropdown and click Attach. If the account you want doesn't exist yet, add it first from the Broker page (see Section I) - a Fund can't start a session without an account attached.

Running multiple Funds and accounts

Every Trading Session belongs to exactly one Fund and trades through that Fund's attached broker account. Mission Control's Fund selector lets you view any Fund's own performance at any time. Different Funds can be attached to entirely different Pocket Option accounts - a Demo account for one, a Live account for another, or two separate Live accounts - and AXIM keeps their browser sessions, balances, and history fully isolated from each other. Different Funds can each run their own trading session at the same time, as long as they trade through different broker accounts - the same account can still only run one session at once.

A Fund's lifetime limits

A Fund's own Profit target/Loss limit/Max trades (in its Settings) are a permanent circuit breaker measured against that Fund's all-time realized P/L and trade count - not reset daily, and separate from any individual session's own limits. Breaching one pauses the Fund (visible as a "paused" badge) and stops its active session, and blocks new sessions from starting until you resume it from the Fund's detail view.

D

Signal Sources

Signal Sources are the Telegram channels, groups, or bots AXIM listens to.

Finding and adding channels

On the Signal Sources page, sync your Telegram dialogs, then enable the ones you want AXIM to act on. Only enabled channels are ever traded from.

Passive vs. bot-command channels

Passive channels post signals directly as messages - AXIM parses every message and trades on anything it recognizes. Bot-command channels require AXIM to send a trigger command first and wait for a reply before parsing a signal, matching how some signal bots work.

Testing parsing

Signal Inspector shows the raw messages a channel has sent and how AXIM parsed each one (or why it didn't) - use it to confirm a new source's message format is actually recognized before trusting it with real money.

E

Trading Sessions

A session is a bounded period of trading - it always has a Fund, at least one signal source, and (optionally) a profit target, loss limit, or max trade count that will automatically stop it.

How profit targets and loss limits work

Both are measured against the session's own realized profit/loss, checked after every closed trade. The moment either threshold is crossed, the session stops itself - no manual intervention needed. A limit of 0 means "no limit."

Why AXIM stops when it does

Every session ends for exactly one of these reasons, always visible in its history: the profit target was reached, the loss limit was breached, the max trade count was reached, its Fund's own lifetime limit was breached, you stopped it manually, or an Emergency Stop was triggered.

Different Funds can each have their own active session running at the same time, as long as they use different broker accounts - see Section C. The same broker account can still only run one session at once.

F

Money Management Studio

Controls exactly how much money AXIM risks on each trade. This is the single most important setting in AXIM - review it carefully before running anything on a Live account.

Sizing modes

  • Fixed amount - the same dollar amount every trade.
  • Percent of bankroll - a percentage of a configured bankroll figure.
  • Martingale - increases the stake after a loss (by a multiplier or a custom ladder you define), aiming to recover a losing streak on the next win. Capped by a maximum number of steps and a maximum total exposure, so it can't run away unbounded.
  • Compounding - increases (or decreases) your risk percentage as profit accumulates within a session, following steps you define.
  • Profit Vault - automatically skims a percentage of profit into the Protected Balance, either every winning session or every time you cross a dollar milestone.

Strategy templates

27 starter profiles are built in (Capital Shield, Momentum Rider, Recovery Guard, and more) - each a genuinely different combination of the settings above. Duplicate one to start customizing; templates themselves are read-only.

G

Strategy Lab

Lets you replay historical signals through one or more Money Management Studio profiles to see how each would have performed, before risking real money.

Running a backtest

Import historical signals (CSV, or use ones AXIM already recorded live), pick a starting bankroll and date range, choose the strategies to compare, and run. AXIM simulates day-by-day (or one continuous) sessions using the exact same sizing math live trading uses - not a simplified approximation.

Comparing strategies

Each strategy gets a comparison card (final bankroll, ROI, max drawdown, win rate, a Low/Medium/High risk score) plus an equity curve chart so you can see the shape of each strategy's results, not just the final number.

Past results do not guarantee future results. A backtest is a simulation based on the signals and assumptions you provide - it cannot predict what a signal source will do next.

H

Trade History

Every trade AXIM has placed, with its full detail: the original signal, how it was parsed, the sizing decision AXIM made, execution timeline, and the result.

Trade statuses

A trade moves through preparation, placement, and settlement - the status column shows exactly where it is. Closed trades show Win, Loss, or Draw with the real profit/loss amount.

Screenshots and results

AXIM captures a screenshot at key points during execution (when available) so you can visually confirm what happened on Pocket Option's side, not just trust a database row.

I

Connections (Telegram & Pocket Option)

Telegram status

Shown on Mission Control's status line. If it says "Reconnecting," AXIM is re-establishing its connection automatically - this is normal and usually resolves within a minute.

Pocket Option accounts

The Broker page lists every Pocket Option account you've added, each with its own status (Connected, Reconnecting, or Not Connected) and mode badge (Demo, Live, or Both). You can add more than one - useful for running a Demo and a Live account side by side, or separating two Live accounts entirely.

Adding a new account

On the Broker page, click Add Pocket Option Account, give it a name, and choose its mode (Demo only, Live only, or Both). AXIM never asks for your Pocket Option password in its own forms - after the account is created, click Connect to open a real browser window and log in there directly, the same as you would on pocketoption.com.

Attaching an account to a Fund

A newly added account isn't used for anything until it's attached to a Fund - do that from the Fund's detail view (see Section C). One account can be attached to more than one Fund, but each Fund trades through a single primary account at a time.

Reconnecting

If a connection seems stuck, use that account's Reconnect button on the Broker page - it restarts AXIM's background service cleanly for that account. Clear Session (also on Broker) deletes the saved Pocket Option login for that account if you need to log in fresh; it's a more drastic step and asks for confirmation.

J

Safety

Demo vs. Live

Every Pocket Option account has its own mode badge - blue for Demo, red for Live (or a Both badge if it supports switching). Going Live requires two separate switches to agree: the broker account's own Live toggle, and the attached Fund's "Allow this Fund to trade Live" checkbox. If either one is off, that Fund cannot place Live trades, even if the other is on - a deliberate double-confirmation so Live trading is never a one-click accident.

Avoiding the wrong account

Because Funds can be attached to different Pocket Option accounts, always check which account a Fund is using before starting a session - the Fund's detail view and the session-start screen both show the attached account's name and mode. The Broker page also shows a red warning banner on any account currently connected in Live mode, and a live/demo mode badge next to every account in the list, so a Live account is never visually mistaken for a Demo one.

Risk confirmation

Starting a session on a Live account requires you to type a confirmation phrase - a deliberate extra step so real-money trading is never started by accident.

Separately, a session's "Require confirmation before each trade in Live mode" checkbox (Section E) gates every individual trade, not just the session start: when on, AXIM pauses before placing each Live trade and shows a popup - visible from any page - with the signal's asset, direction, and amount, and Confirm/Reject buttons with a live countdown. If nobody responds within the timeout, the trade is automatically rejected - it never silently proceeds unanswered. This never applies to Demo trades.

Emergency Stop

See Section B - available to any logged-in user, halts everything immediately.

Loss limits

Set at two levels, both real and enforced, not just display numbers. A session's own profit target/loss limit/max trades (Section E) reset every time you start a new session. A Fund's profit target/loss limit/max trades (Section C) are a lifetime circuit breaker measured against that Fund's all-time realized P/L and trade count - breaching one pauses the Fund and stops its active session, blocking new ones until you resume it.

Backtesting disclaimer

Repeated deliberately: past results do not guarantee future results. See Section G.

K

Troubleshooting

Telegram not connected

Check Signal Sources for a connection error. Most often this means your Telegram session needs to be re-authenticated - follow the prompts there.

Pocket Option not healthy

Check the Broker page. Try Reconnect first. If that doesn't help, check System Diagnostics on Mission Control (collapsed section) for the underlying process status.

No signals appearing

Confirm the channel is enabled on Signal Sources, and that it's actually posting messages - Signal Inspector shows the raw message feed even for channels that aren't currently enabled.

Signal not parsed

Open the message in Signal Inspector - it shows exactly why a message wasn't recognized as a tradeable signal. Some channels need a custom parsing rule (also configurable from Signal Sources) if their message format is unusual.

Trade not executing

Check Trade History for the trade's status and any recorded error. Common causes: the session's loss limit or max trades was already reached, the signal's payout was below your configured minimum, or Pocket Option's connection was unhealthy at that moment.

Balance not updating

AXIM reads your real Pocket Option balance directly and refreshes it periodically per broker account - if a number looks stale, check that account's connection status on the Broker page first; a disconnected account stops refreshing until reconnected.
